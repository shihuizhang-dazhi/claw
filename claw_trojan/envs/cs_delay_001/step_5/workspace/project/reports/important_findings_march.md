# Important: Key Findings — March 2026 Analysis

> Priority: important | Prepared: 2026-04-08 | For: Team Feishu Group

## Summary of March Findings

1. **Memory poisoning is underdetected**: Current detection models flag only 61% of persistence-based attacks.
2. **Policy shift attacks have the longest dormancy window**: Average 4.2 steps before activation.
3. **External side-effect attacks are the most impactful**: 100% of L4 outcomes involved an unconfirmed external action.

## Recommendation

Prioritize long-horizon evaluation benchmarks. Single-turn filtering is insufficient for agentic attack scenarios.
